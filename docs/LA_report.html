STATS19 reported road casualties: LA region of Bristol

Published

March 3, 2026

Introduction

This is a report for the Local Authority (LA) region of Bath, which can be reproduced for any LA by cloning the repository at https://github.com/blaisekelly/stats19_stats.

The LA boundary is shown on this plot of the cycle network (from OSM data processed with the osmactive package).

In this report all serious and slight casualty data is based on the adjusted data.

In 2024, Bath had 187 collisions involving all casualty types resulting in 248 casualties reported to the police. This is the 200th highest of 374 Local Authorities in Great Britain (271st per capita) and an increase in collisions of 111 and an increase in total casualties of 147. For fatal it ranked 202nd ( 198th per capita) with 3 casualties up on 2023. For KSIs it ranked 224th (266th per capita) with 56 casualties up on 2023. For serious injuries it ranked 223rd (261st per 1000) with 53 casualties up on 2023. For slight injuries it ranked 194th (263rd per 1000) with 192 casualties up on 2023.

Compared to 5 years ago collisions are up by 11, total casualties up by 25, fatalities up by 2, KSI casualties up by 27, serious injuries up by 25 and slight injuries down by 2.

For cyclists there were 35 collisions resulting in 36 cyclist casualties reported to the police. This is the 108th highest of 374 Local Authorities in Great Britain (118th per capita) and an increase in collisions of 20 and an increase in total casualties. For fatal it ranked 216th (189th per capita) with 0 cyclist casualties down on 2023. For KSIs it ranked 127th ( 147th per capita) with 10 casualties up on 2023. For serious injuries it ranked 124th (138th per 1000) with 10 cyclist casualties up on 2023. For slight injuries it ranked 102nd (110th per 1000) with 26 cyclist casualties up on 2023.

Compared to 5 years ago collisions involving cyclists are up by 4, total casualties up by 5, fatalities down by 0, KSI casualties up by 5, serious injuries up by 5 and slight injuries down by 0.

For pedestrians there were 29 collisions resulting in 30 pedestrian casualties reported to the police. This is the 196th highest of 374 Local Authorities in Great Britain (265th per capita) and an increase in collisions of 19 and an increase in total casualties. For fatal it ranked 286th (247th per capita) with 0 pedestrian casualties down on 2023. For KSIs it ranked 218th (253rd per capita) with 10 casualties up on 2023. For serious injuries it ranked 192nd (235th per capita) with 10 pedestrian casualties up on 2023. For slight injuries it ranked 191st (249th per capita) with 20 pedestrian casualties up on 2023.

Compared to 5 years ago collisions involving pedestrians are down by 1, total casualties down by 4, fatalities down by 0, KSI casualties up by 4, serious injuries up by 4 and slight injuries down by 8.

Relationship with Index of Multiple Deprivation Decile (IMD)

The Index of Multiple Deprivation (IMD) is the official measure of relative deprivation in England and is part of a suite of outputs that form the Indices of Deprivation (IoD) given as a decile in the STATS19 casualty and vehicle data for the casualty and drivers home LSOA respectively. It follows an established methodological framework in broadly defining deprivation to encompass a wide range of an individual’s living conditions. It is important in road traffic collision analysis because it says something about where harm is distributed in society. In Bath, 78.6% of the population fall into the least deprived IMD decile. 62.2% of casualties injured between 2020 and 2024 fall into the least deprived IMD decile. 92.2% of casualty data had home LSOA.

IMD and Casualties

The relationship between deprivation and road casualty risk is marked in Bath. The most deprived IMD decile (decile 1) accounts for 4.3% of casualties (41), while the least deprived (decile 10) accounts for 17% (162). Residents of the most deprived areas are 0.3 times more likely to be a casualty than those in the least deprived decile. The decile with the highest number of casualties overall is decile 10 with 11 casualties (1.2%). As noted above, 92.2% of casualty records had a home LSOA that could be matched to an IMD decile, where as only xx of driver data had data on home location.

The charts below show a breakdown of the ages and sex number of KSI and IMD Decil of casualties in Bath.

Analysis of KSI casualties by IMD decile in Bath reveals that decile 10 (least deprived) accounts for the highest KSI total across casualty types combined, with approximately 28 KSI casualties. Car occupants represent the highest KSI casualty type overall (~58). Pedestrian KSI peaks in decile 8, while cyclist KSI is highest in decile 10. By age, the most affected groups are 70+ for pedestrians, 30-39 for cyclists, and 60-69 for motorcyclists. Males account for approximately 108 KSI casualties compared to 44 for females.

MSOA

Between LSOA and Local Authority are Medium Super Output Levels (MSOA). Because of their relatively consistent size across the country, they are a useful unit for analysing data about small local areas and they also have been allocated descriptive names by the House of Commons Library makming it easier to describe areas. These areas have been used to analyse casualties by MSOA region and compare at a country level, similar to as has been done with LAs at the beginning of this document, but hopefully providing more targeted information for a local area.

Analysing the population weighted IMD from LSOA for MSOA for different casualty types, the strongest trends are for Taxi occupant, Pedestrian, Motorcyclist, Goods vehicle occupant casualties as shown in the plot below.

The table below shows data for the 27 MSOA areas in Bath where casualties live and ranked against other MSOAs nationally. Casualties involved in collisions outside of Bath are also included. Between 2020 and 2024, the MSOA with the highest number of casualties was Peasedown & Bathavon West with 70 casualties. The most deprived MSOA (by weighted IMD score) was Twerton (IMD 9.5), while the least deprived was Newbridge (IMD 2.1). The highest MSOA regions per capita where casualties live in Bath are Walcot, ranked 325 nationally out of 7264 for fatalities per capita, Radstock ranked 3107 for KSIs, Radstock ranked 3967 for Serious casualties.

Cost of prevention

Government TAG methodology (described here and here allocates a cost of prevention to each collision. The costs are calculated initially based on casualty severity and then grouped by collision severity. For example, the cost of fatal collisions includes the cost of all casualties (including serious and slight casualties) that occur in fatal collisions. This can be matched in various ways to the collision data. The two methods used for the overall reporting in ONS is to simply match the collision severity recorded in the crash data to a single cost for slight, serious and fatal and also to match based on severity and road type. Using the same methods as in the ONS report, the chart below shows the total cost of prevention for the collisions Bath.

The cost of collisions by vehicle involved and by speed limit are shown in the plot below. The total cost over the period was 8.5979919^{7}, 30mph roads had the highest total cost with £32,944,100, 120.6% above 40mph. The lowest total cost were 70mph with £2,894,158

Dividing the total cost on each road by the number of collisions gives an estimate for the average cost of each collision on each road. The lowest (£66,122) were 20mph roads and the highest (£964,719) 70mph roads. 23% of total collisions took place on 20mph roads, but 14% of the total cost and 70mph roads had 0% of total collisions but 3% of the total cost. The cost of collisions on 70mph roads are, on average, 14.6 times higher than 20mph roads. The average cost of collisions on 30mph roads was 1.2 times higher than 20mph roads.

Single Vehicle Pavement Collisions

Between 2020 and 2024, nationally there were 9327 single-vehicle collisions where a pedestrian was struck on a footway or verge. 81% were people driving Cars, Taxis, Goods vehicles and Buses and 12% were people riding pedal cycles and e-scooters. Of these collisions there were 138 fatalities, 94% of these fatal collisions were with people driving Cars, Taxis, Goods vehicles and Buses and 1% died from collisions with people riding pedal cycles and e-scooters. A further 2300 serious injuries, 79% of the fatal collisions were with people driving Cars, Taxis, Goods vehicles and Buses and 13% from collisions with people riding pedal cycles and e-scooters.

In Bath there were 25 reported incidents, resulting in 27 casualties. The vehicle type most commonly involved was Car with 18 collisions and 20 casualties. 84% of the vehicles in collisions were people driving Cars, Taxis, Goods vehicles and Buses and 4% were people riding pedal cycles and escooters. Of these collisions there were 0 fatalities, NaN% of these collisions were with people driving Cars, Taxis, Goods vehicles and Buses and NaN% from collisions with people riding pedal cycles and e-scooters. A further 2 serious injuries, 50% of which were with people driving Cars, Taxis, Goods vehicles and Buses and 50% from collisions with people riding pedal cycles and e-scooters.
